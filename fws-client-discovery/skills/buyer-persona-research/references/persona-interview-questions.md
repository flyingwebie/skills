# Persona Interview Questions, Client Meeting Framework

## Purpose
Questions to extract persona data from client meetings. These can be used as a prep guide before meetings or as a framework for parsing meeting transcriptions.

## Section 1: Who Are Your Customers?

### Direct Questions
1. Describe your ideal customer in one sentence.
2. What job titles do your best customers hold?
3. What size companies are they? (employees, revenue)
4. What industries do they come from?
5. How do they typically find you? (referral, search, social, etc.)

### Probe Deeper
6. Think of your 3 best customers ever, what do they have in common?
7. Think of your worst customers, what made them bad fits?
8. Is there a type of customer you'd love more of but can't seem to attract?

## Section 2: Pain Points & Triggers

### Direct Questions
9. What problem are customers trying to solve when they come to you?
10. What event or situation triggers them to start looking for a solution?
11. What have they typically tried before finding you?
12. What's the cost of NOT solving this problem?

### Probe Deeper
13. When a new lead contacts you, what do they say in the first message/call?
14. What's the most common frustration they express?
15. What misconceptions do they have about your industry?

## Section 3: Decision Process

### Direct Questions
16. How long does it typically take from first contact to signed deal?
17. Who else is involved in the decision? (boss, partner, committee)
18. What's their typical budget range?
19. What are the top 3 reasons people choose you over competitors?
20. What are the top 3 reasons people DON'T choose you?

### Probe Deeper
21. What questions do prospects always ask?
22. What's the #1 objection you hear?
23. What makes someone say "yes" on the spot vs. "let me think about it"?
24. Do they compare you to specific competitors? Which ones?

## Section 4: Value & Outcomes

### Direct Questions
25. What results do your customers get? (specific metrics if possible)
26. How quickly do they see results?
27. What do customers say in testimonials or reviews?
28. What's the ROI story you tell prospects?

### Probe Deeper
29. Beyond the tangible result, how does your service make them feel?
30. What would your customers say if I asked "why do you stay with [client]?"
31. Has anyone ever come back after leaving? Why?

## Section 5: Content & Communication

### Direct Questions
32. Where do your customers hang out online? (LinkedIn, forums, Facebook groups)
33. What publications or blogs do they read?
34. Do they prefer email, phone, text, or in-person?
35. What type of content would be most useful to them?

### Probe Deeper
36. If you could send one piece of content to every prospect, what would it be?
37. What's the one thing you wish every prospect understood before the first call?

## Parsing Transcriptions

When parsing meeting transcriptions (rather than conducting live interviews), look for:

### High-Signal Phrases
- "Our typical customer is..."
- "The people who love us..."
- "We struggle with..."
- "They always ask about..."
- "The biggest objection is..."
- "What makes us different is..."
- "Our best results have been..."
- "The worst fits are..."

### Implicit Signals
- Language about pricing sensitivity
- Mentions of competitors (positive or negative)
- Emotional language about customers
- Stories about specific customer wins or losses
- Frustration about types of inquiries
- Excitement about particular customer segments

### Red Flags (Assumption Warnings)
- "Everyone is our customer" → needs narrowing
- No specific metrics on results → probe for outcomes
- Can't articulate why customers choose them → positioning gap
- Only talks about features, not outcomes → needs value reframing
