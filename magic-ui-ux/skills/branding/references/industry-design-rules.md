# Industry Design Rules

Niche-to-design-system mapping rules. Each industry defines color psychology, typography direction, UI style tendency, component patterns, spacing tendency, and mood keywords.

These rules provide **directions**, not exact values. The branding skill uses these directions to generate specific hex codes, font names, and token values contextually for each project.

---

## SaaS / Tech

**Color psychology:** Blues and purples convey innovation and reliability. Cool tones dominate. Primary should feel modern and trustworthy -- think deep blue or electric purple. Secondary complements with a cooler neutral or accent. CTA must pop: bright green, orange, or contrasting warm tone against cool primary. Background is clean white or very light gray.

**Typography direction:** Sans-serif for everything. Clean, geometric fonts signal modernity. Heading and body can share a family at different weights, or pair a geometric heading (Space Grotesk, Inter, DM Sans) with a humanist body (Source Sans 3, Nunito Sans). Medium to bold headings, regular body.

**UI style tendency:** Minimalism or Gradient-Rich. SaaS products benefit from clean interfaces with occasional gradient accents on hero sections or CTAs.

**Component patterns:**
- Header: sticky-minimal (logo + nav + single CTA button)
- Hero: centered-text or split-content (headline left, product screenshot right)
- CTA: bold-contrast (high-saturation button on clean background)
- Footer: minimal-links (organized columns, not overwhelming)

**Spacing tendency:** Generous. Whitespace signals premium and reduces cognitive load for complex product explanations.

**Mood keywords:** modern, clean, trustworthy, innovative, professional

## Fintech / Finance

**Color psychology:** Blues and greens are dominant -- blue for trust and stability, green for growth and money association. Deep navy or teal as primary. Secondary in muted green or slate. CTA in a warm contrasting tone (coral, amber) to break the cool palette. Background is white or very light cool gray. Avoid red except for error states -- it signals danger in finance.

**Typography direction:** Sans-serif dominates for clarity and professionalism. Pair a strong geometric heading font (Inter, DM Sans, Plus Jakarta Sans) with a highly legible body font (Source Sans 3, IBM Plex Sans). Some premium fintech brands use a serif heading (Libre Baskerville, DM Serif Display) for gravitas paired with sans-serif body.

**UI style tendency:** Minimalism. Finance demands clarity above all. Data-heavy interfaces need clean grids and breathing room. Occasional Glassmorphism for dashboard cards.

**Component patterns:**
- Header: sticky-minimal (trust signals in nav -- security badges, login)
- Hero: split-content (value prop left, dashboard preview or phone mockup right)
- CTA: bold-contrast (clear action, no ambiguity)
- Footer: mega-footer (regulatory links, compliance, multiple resource sections)

**Spacing tendency:** Generous to moderate. Readability is critical for financial information. Never feel cramped.

**Mood keywords:** trustworthy, stable, secure, precise, authoritative

## E-commerce / Retail

**Color psychology:** Varies widely by product type. Warm tones (orange, coral) drive urgency and action. Primary can be bold and brand-specific. Secondary supports categorization. CTA must be unmissable -- high-contrast, often orange, red, or bright green for "Add to Cart." Background is white for product photography clarity. Dark variants work for luxury sub-segments.

**Typography direction:** Sans-serif for most retail (Poppins, Outfit, Nunito). Bold headings grab attention amid product noise. Body must be highly legible at small sizes for product descriptions. E-commerce for fashion may use a display serif heading (Playfair Display, Cormorant Garamond) for editorial flair.

**UI style tendency:** Minimalism for products to shine. Some brands lean Neubrutalism for bold, attention-grabbing storefronts. Clean grid layouts are essential.

**Component patterns:**
- Header: bold-branded (prominent search, cart icon, category nav)
- Hero: full-bleed-image (product photography or seasonal campaign)
- CTA: bold-contrast (unmissable "Shop Now" or "Add to Cart")
- Footer: mega-footer (categories, policies, customer service, social links)

**Spacing tendency:** Moderate to tight. Efficient use of space for product grids while maintaining scannability.

**Mood keywords:** vibrant, actionable, inviting, dynamic, accessible

## Healthcare / Wellness

**Color psychology:** Greens, soft blues, and warm whites create calm and trust. Primary in sage green, teal, or soft blue. Secondary in warm neutrals (cream, sand) or complementary green-blue tones. CTA in a grounding warm tone (terracotta, warm coral) that feels approachable, not aggressive. Background is warm white or very light sage. Avoid harsh clinical whites.

**Typography direction:** Rounded sans-serif for approachability (Nunito, Quicksand, DM Sans). Alternatively, a humanist serif heading (Merriweather, Lora) paired with a clean sans-serif body conveys authority with warmth. Avoid overly clinical or tech-forward fonts.

**UI style tendency:** Soft UI or Minimalism. Rounded corners, soft shadows, calming whitespace. The interface should feel like a deep breath.

**Component patterns:**
- Header: transparent-overlay or sticky-minimal (calming, not aggressive)
- Hero: centered-text (reassuring headline, soft background image or illustration)
- CTA: subtle-inline (warm, inviting buttons -- not salesy)
- Footer: centered-simple (clean, trust-building, contact info prominent)

**Spacing tendency:** Very generous. Breathing room is literal for wellness brands -- space creates calm.

**Mood keywords:** calming, trustworthy, nurturing, clean, professional

## Education / EdTech

**Color psychology:** Blues for trust and focus, with energetic accents (yellow, orange, teal) for engagement. Primary in medium blue or teal. Secondary in a warm accent (yellow, orange) for interactive elements and highlights. CTA in contrasting warm tone. Background is clean white. Accent colors can be more saturated for younger audiences, more muted for professional/adult education.

**Typography direction:** Friendly sans-serif for accessibility (Nunito, Poppins, Lexend). Heading should be inviting and clear, not intimidating. Body font must be optimized for extended reading (Atkinson Hyperlegible, Source Sans 3). K-12 skews playful (Baloo 2, Fredoka), adult ed skews professional (Inter, DM Sans).

**UI style tendency:** Minimalism with colorful accents. Clean interface with strategic color to guide attention and signal interactivity. Gamified elements for younger audiences.

**Component patterns:**
- Header: sticky-minimal (clear navigation, prominent sign-up/login)
- Hero: split-content (compelling headline with illustration or student imagery)
- CTA: bold-contrast (clear enrollment or sign-up action)
- Footer: minimal-links (resources, support, social)

**Spacing tendency:** Generous. Learning interfaces need room to breathe -- cramped layouts increase cognitive load.

**Mood keywords:** engaging, accessible, trustworthy, friendly, clear

## Real Estate / Property

**Color psychology:** Deep blues and greens for trust and stability, paired with warm metallics or earth tones for luxury feel. Primary in navy, dark teal, or forest green. Secondary in warm neutral (gold, bronze, warm gray). CTA in a warm contrasting tone (gold, amber, or a bright accent). Background is white or light warm gray. Dark mode variants work well for luxury properties.

**Typography direction:** Serif headings for sophistication and establishment (Playfair Display, DM Serif Display, Cormorant Garamond). Clean sans-serif body for property details (Inter, Source Sans 3). The serif-sans pairing signals "premium but practical."

**UI style tendency:** Editorial or Minimalism. Large property imagery drives layout. Clean grids for listings, editorial treatment for feature properties.

**Component patterns:**
- Header: transparent-overlay (over hero property imagery)
- Hero: full-bleed-image (stunning property photography)
- CTA: subtle-inline (search bar or "Schedule a Tour" -- not pushy)
- Footer: mega-footer (neighborhoods, property types, agent info, legal)

**Spacing tendency:** Generous. Properties need room to breathe visually. Dense listing grids use moderate spacing.

**Mood keywords:** sophisticated, trustworthy, premium, established, aspirational

## Food / Restaurant

**Color psychology:** Warm colors dominate -- reds, oranges, warm yellows stimulate appetite. Earth tones (terracotta, olive, warm brown) for artisanal/organic positioning. Primary is warm and inviting. Secondary in complementary warm tone or earthy neutral. CTA in high-contrast warm (deep red, vibrant orange). Background is warm white or cream. Dark backgrounds for upscale dining.

**Typography direction:** Display or script heading fonts for personality (Playfair Display, Cormorant Garamond for upscale; Fredoka, Baloo 2 for casual). Clean sans-serif body for menus and descriptions (DM Sans, Nunito). Food brands thrive on typographic personality -- generic fonts feel bland.

**UI style tendency:** Editorial for upscale dining (dramatic typography, full-bleed food photography). Minimalism for fast-casual. Neubrutalism for trendy/street food brands.

**Component patterns:**
- Header: transparent-overlay (over food photography hero)
- Hero: full-bleed-image (appetizing food photography is essential)
- CTA: bold-contrast ("Order Now", "Reserve a Table" -- warm, inviting)
- Footer: centered-simple (hours, location, social links, reservation link)

**Spacing tendency:** Moderate. Dense enough for menus and multi-item layouts, but hero sections need generous space for photography.

**Mood keywords:** warm, appetizing, inviting, authentic, flavorful

## Creative Agency / Portfolio

**Color psychology:** High contrast and bold choices signal creativity. Black and white as primary palette with a single electric accent color (neon green, electric blue, hot pink, vibrant orange). Or deep dark backgrounds with luminous accent colors. The accent color IS the brand. Background often dark or pure white for maximum portfolio contrast.

**Typography direction:** Strong display headings that make a statement (Space Grotesk, Syne, Clash Display, Cabinet Grotesk). Body in a clean contrasting sans-serif (Inter, DM Sans). Typography IS the design for agencies -- oversized headings, creative kerning, mixed weights.

**UI style tendency:** Dark Mode Premium or Neubrutalism. Agencies showcase creative range through their own site. Bold, opinionated design choices over safe defaults.

**Component patterns:**
- Header: sticky-minimal (logo + burger menu for clean portfolio browsing)
- Hero: centered-text (bold statement, oversized typography, subtle animation)
- CTA: subtle-inline or floating-sticky (the work speaks for itself)
- Footer: minimal-links (contact, social, brief bio)

**Spacing tendency:** Very generous. White (or dark) space is a design tool. Let work breathe.

**Mood keywords:** bold, creative, cutting-edge, expressive, distinctive

## Legal / Professional Services

**Color psychology:** Deep blues, dark grays, and burgundy convey authority, trust, and tradition. Primary in navy or dark charcoal. Secondary in muted burgundy, gold, or warm gray. CTA in a warm but restrained tone (muted gold, warm accent). Background is white or light warm gray. The palette should feel established, not trendy.

**Typography direction:** Serif headings are almost mandatory for gravitas (Libre Baskerville, Merriweather, EB Garamond, Crimson Pro). Body in a highly legible serif (for traditional firms) or clean sans-serif (for modern firms) like Source Sans 3, Inter. Professional services need to feel authoritative through type.

**UI style tendency:** Minimalism with editorial undertones. Clean, structured layouts with prominent text. No decorative flourishes -- let the content demonstrate expertise.

**Component patterns:**
- Header: classic-nav (firm name prominent, practice areas in nav, contact CTA)
- Hero: centered-text (clear value proposition, professional -- not flashy)
- CTA: subtle-inline (schedule consultation, not aggressive sales)
- Footer: mega-footer (practice areas, attorney listings, office locations, legal disclaimers)

**Spacing tendency:** Generous. Density signals chaos in legal -- space signals composure and thoroughness.

**Mood keywords:** authoritative, trustworthy, established, professional, composed

## Travel / Hospitality

**Color psychology:** Warm and aspirational palette. Ocean blues and sandy neutrals for beach/resort. Deep greens and earth tones for adventure/eco travel. Primary in a destination-inspired color (ocean teal, sunset coral, forest green). Secondary in warm neutral (sand, warm gray, cream). CTA in warm energetic tone (coral, amber, sunset orange). Background is warm white. Photography does the heavy color lifting.

**Typography direction:** Elegant serif headings for luxury travel (Cormorant Garamond, Playfair Display). Friendly sans-serif for adventure/budget travel (Poppins, Nunito, DM Sans). Body always in a readable sans-serif. The heading font sets the travel tier (luxury vs accessible).

**UI style tendency:** Editorial for luxury (magazine-style layouts, dramatic imagery). Minimalism for booking-focused platforms. Gradient-Rich for modern travel startups.

**Component patterns:**
- Header: transparent-overlay (over stunning destination photography)
- Hero: full-bleed-image or video-background (destinations sell visually)
- CTA: bold-contrast ("Book Now", "Explore Destinations" -- action-oriented)
- Footer: mega-footer (destinations, trip types, company info, trust badges)

**Spacing tendency:** Generous for hero and editorial sections. Moderate for search results and booking flows.

**Mood keywords:** aspirational, warm, adventurous, inviting, wanderlust

## Fitness / Sports

**Color psychology:** High-energy, high-contrast palettes. Primary in bold, energetic tones -- electric blue, vibrant red, neon green, or deep black. Secondary in a contrasting high-saturation accent. CTA in maximum-contrast bright tone (neon green on dark, bright orange on light). Background can be dark (intensity) or white (clean performance). Sports brands thrive on contrast and energy.

**Typography direction:** Bold, condensed sans-serif headings for impact (Oswald, Bebas Neue, Anton, Barlow Condensed). Body in clean readable sans-serif (Inter, DM Sans, Source Sans 3). Fitness typography is about IMPACT -- heavy weights, tight spacing, uppercase options.

**UI style tendency:** Dark Mode Premium or Gradient-Rich. High contrast, bold imagery, energetic gradients. Minimalism works for premium fitness brands (think upscale gym vs CrossFit box).

**Component patterns:**
- Header: sticky-minimal (bold logo, minimal nav, strong sign-up CTA)
- Hero: full-bleed-image or video-background (action shots, intensity)
- CTA: bold-contrast (high-energy "Start Your Journey", "Join Now")
- Footer: minimal-links (programs, schedule, location, social)

**Spacing tendency:** Tight to moderate. Energy and intensity -- not empty space. Hero sections get more room, but content sections pack information densely.

**Mood keywords:** energetic, bold, powerful, motivating, intense

## Luxury / Fashion

**Color psychology:** Black, white, and metallic accents define luxury. Primary in black or very deep color (midnight navy, deep burgundy). Secondary in gold, silver, or rose gold metallic tone. CTA is subtle -- luxury does not scream, it whispers. Background in pure white or deep black. The palette is intentionally restrained: fewer colors = more exclusive.

**Typography direction:** Serif headings are the standard for luxury (Playfair Display, Cormorant Garamond, Bodoni Moda). Ultra-thin or high-contrast serif weights signal refinement. Body in a light sans-serif (Montserrat Light, Raleway, DM Sans). Some modern luxury brands use all-caps thin sans-serif headings (Josefin Sans, Poiret One) for contemporary edge.

**UI style tendency:** Editorial or Dark Mode Premium. Magazine-style layouts with dramatic whitespace. Full-bleed photography. Typography as art. Every element feels curated, not computed.

**Component patterns:**
- Header: transparent-overlay or sticky-minimal (minimal links, brand mark centered)
- Hero: full-bleed-image (editorial photography, minimal text overlay)
- CTA: subtle-inline (understated "Discover", "View Collection" -- never "Buy Now!")
- Footer: centered-simple (minimal information, maximally elegant)

**Spacing tendency:** Extremely generous. Luxury is defined by what you leave out. White space is the most expensive design element.

**Mood keywords:** exclusive, refined, elegant, premium, aspirational

## Cross-Industry Rules

These apply regardless of industry:

1. **WCAG AA contrast**: Text color against background must achieve 4.5:1 contrast ratio minimum. CTA text against CTA background must achieve 4.5:1.
2. **CTA distinctiveness**: CTA color must be visually distinct from primary -- never the same or too similar. It needs to draw the eye independently.
3. **Font pairing harmony**: Heading and body fonts should complement, not compete. One display + one workhorse. Never two display fonts.
4. **Semantic color consistency**: Success is always in the green family, warning in amber/yellow, error in red. These do not change with industry.
5. **Google Fonts only**: All font families must be available on Google Fonts for universal web access.
