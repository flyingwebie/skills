# Copy Generation Guide

Section-by-section copy patterns organized by section type, with psychology-aligned writing techniques, length guidelines, and anti-patterns.

## Section Copy Patterns

### Hero

**Copy elements needed:**
- Headline (primary hook)
- Subheading (supporting context)
- CTA label (primary action)
- Optional: secondary CTA label, supporting line

**Psychology-aligned writing patterns:**

| Psychology Skill | Headline Approach | Example Pattern |
|------------------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Curiosity gap | Open with a provocative question or surprising stat that creates an information gap | "What if your [process] took 10x less time?" |
| Loss aversion | Frame around what the reader risks missing or losing | "Stop losing [valuable thing] to [common problem]" |
| Social proof | Lead with collective validation | "[X,000] teams already [achieved outcome]" |
| Cognitive fluency | Use simple, instantly understood language | "[Do thing]. [Get result]. [That simple]." |
| Halo effect | Lead with your strongest credential or visual | "Award-winning [thing] trusted by [impressive names]" |

**Length guidelines:**
- Headline: 6-12 words
- Subheading: 15-25 words
- CTA label: 2-5 words

**Anti-patterns:**
- "Welcome to our website" (zero information, zero hook)
- Headline longer than 15 words (loses scanning impact)
- Passive voice in CTA ("Get started" beats "Getting started is easy")
- Multiple competing CTAs in the same hero

### Features

**Copy elements needed:**
- Section headline
- Feature titles (3-6 features)
- Feature descriptions (1-2 sentences each)
- Optional: section subheading

**Psychology-aligned writing patterns:**

| Psychology Skill | Writing Approach |
|------------------|-----------------|
| Cognitive load | Group features into 3-4 categories max; use parallel sentence structure |
| Hick's law | Present features in priority order; limit to 3-6 items |
| Progressive disclosure | Lead with benefit, detail on hover/expand |
| Cognitive fluency | Feature title = benefit, not technical name |

**Length guidelines:**
- Section headline: 4-8 words
- Feature title: 2-5 words
- Feature description: 15-30 words

**Anti-patterns:**
- Feature names that are internal jargon ("Multi-tenant architecture" vs. "Works for your whole team")
- Descriptions that list specs without benefits
- More than 6 features visible at once (cognitive overload)

---

### Testimonials / Social Proof

**Copy elements needed:**
- Section headline
- Testimonial quotes (2-4)
- Attribution (name, role, company)
- Optional: metric callouts, logo labels

**Psychology-aligned writing patterns:**

| Psychology Skill | Writing Approach |
|------------------|-----------------|
| Social proof | Use specific outcomes and numbers in quotes, not vague praise |
| Trust | Include full attribution (name, title, company); specificity builds credibility |
| Halo effect | Lead with the most recognizable name or brand |
| Cognitive biases | Leverage similarity bias -- testimonials from people like the target audience |

**Length guidelines:**
- Section headline: 3-6 words
- Testimonial quote: 20-50 words (long enough for substance, short enough to scan)
- Attribution: Name, Title at Company

**Anti-patterns:**
- "Great product!" (no substance, feels fake)
- Anonymous testimonials (no attribution destroys trust)
- All testimonials saying the same thing (variety shows breadth)

---

### CTA / Conversion

**Copy elements needed:**
- Headline (action-oriented or outcome-oriented)
- Supporting text (1-2 sentences addressing hesitation)
- Primary CTA label
- Optional: secondary CTA label, reassurance line

**Psychology-aligned writing patterns:**

| Psychology Skill | Writing Approach |
|------------------|-----------------|
| Loss aversion | "Don't miss [outcome]" or "Stop [pain point] today" |
| Fogg behavior model | Reduce perceived effort: "Takes 2 minutes" or "No credit card required" |
| Visual cues / CTA | CTA label states the outcome, not the action: "Get my free report" vs. "Submit" |
| Curiosity gap | "See what [impressive thing] looks like for your [business/project]" |
| Status quo bias | Frame inaction as the risky choice: "Every day without [solution] costs you [thing]" |

**Length guidelines:**
- Headline: 5-10 words
- Supporting text: 15-30 words
- CTA label: 2-5 words
- Reassurance line: 5-12 words

**Anti-patterns:**
- "Submit" as a CTA label (generic, no outcome)
- No supporting text (CTA feels abrupt)
- Multiple equal-weight CTAs (decision paralysis)

### About / Story

**Copy elements needed:**
- Section headline
- Origin story or mission statement (2-4 paragraphs)
- Team highlights (if applicable)
- Optional: values list, timeline milestones

**Psychology-aligned writing patterns:**

| Psychology Skill | Writing Approach |
|------------------|-----------------|
| Trust | Specific founding story with real details beats corporate mission statements |
| Halo effect | Lead with most impressive achievement or credential |
| Social proof | Mention customer count, years in business, notable clients |
| Cognitive fluency | Write in first person plural ("we") for warmth; avoid corporate third person |

**Length guidelines:**
- Section headline: 3-6 words
- Mission/story: 50-150 words
- Team bio: 20-40 words per person

**Anti-patterns:**
- "We are a leading provider of innovative solutions" (says nothing)
- Third-person corporate speak ("The company was founded...")
- No specific details (dates, numbers, names)

### Pricing

**Copy elements needed:**
- Section headline
- Plan names (2-4 tiers)
- Plan descriptions (1 sentence each)
- Feature lists per plan
- CTA labels per plan
- Optional: comparison callouts, "most popular" badge text

**Psychology-aligned writing patterns:**

| Psychology Skill | Writing Approach |
|------------------|-----------------|
| Hick's law | 2-4 plans maximum; highlight one as recommended |
| Cognitive biases (anchoring) | Show highest tier first or use decoy pricing |
| Loss aversion | Frame upgrades as "Don't miss [feature]" not "Upgrade to get [feature]" |
| Status quo bias | Default-select the recommended plan visually |
| Fogg behavior model | "Start free" or "No commitment" reduces friction |

**Length guidelines:**
- Section headline: 3-6 words
- Plan name: 1-3 words
- Plan description: 10-20 words
- Feature list item: 3-8 words

**Anti-patterns:**
- More than 4 visible pricing tiers (decision paralysis)
- Feature lists longer than 8 items (cognitive overload)
- No recommended/highlighted plan (forces unnecessary comparison)

### FAQ

**Copy elements needed:**
- Section headline
- Questions (5-8)
- Answers (2-4 sentences each)

**Psychology-aligned writing patterns:**

| Psychology Skill | Writing Approach |
|------------------|-----------------|
| Trust | Address real objections, not softball questions |
| Cognitive load | Group questions by theme; keep answers concise |
| Progressive disclosure | Questions visible, answers expand on click |
| Loss aversion | Include "What happens if I don't [action]?" style questions |

**Length guidelines:**
- Section headline: 3-6 words
- Question: 5-15 words
- Answer: 30-75 words

**Anti-patterns:**
- Questions nobody actually asks ("What makes us the best?")
- Answers longer than 100 words (defeats the purpose)
- No structure/grouping when more than 6 questions

### Contact

**Copy elements needed:**
- Section headline
- Introductory line
- Form field labels and placeholder text
- Submit button label
- Optional: reassurance text, response time expectation

**Psychology-aligned writing patterns:**

| Psychology Skill | Writing Approach |
|------------------|-----------------|
| Fogg behavior model | Minimize fields (3-5 max); every extra field reduces completion |
| Trust | "We respond within 24 hours" sets expectations and builds reliability |
| Cognitive fluency | Labels above fields, not inside (placeholder text disappears) |
| Visual cues / CTA | Submit button states outcome: "Send my message" not "Submit" |

**Length guidelines:**
- Section headline: 3-6 words
- Intro line: 10-20 words
- Field labels: 1-3 words
- Placeholder text: 3-8 words
- Submit label: 2-5 words

**Anti-patterns:**
- More than 5 form fields (abandonment spikes)
- "Submit" as button text
- No indication of what happens after submission

- Optional: newsletter signup headline and CTA

**Length guidelines:**
- Tagline: 5-12 words
- Link labels: 1-3 words each
- Newsletter CTA: 2-5 words

### Navigation

**Copy elements needed:**
- Menu item labels (5-7 items max)
- Optional: CTA button label in nav

**Psychology-aligned writing patterns:**

| Psychology Skill | Writing Approach |
|------------------|-----------------|
| Hick's law | 5-7 items maximum; prioritize by user intent |
| Cognitive fluency | Use common labels ("Pricing" not "Investment Options") |
| Visual cues / CTA | One standout CTA button in nav (e.g., "Get Started") |

**Anti-patterns:**
- More than 7 top-level nav items
- Jargon labels ("Solutions" when you mean "Products")
- No CTA in navigation

### Blog / Content

**Copy elements needed:**
- Article headline
- Meta description / excerpt
- Subheadings (H2s for scanning)
- Body paragraphs
- Optional: author bio, related post titles

**Psychology-aligned writing patterns:**

| Psychology Skill | Writing Approach |
|------------------|-----------------|
| Curiosity gap | Headlines that create information gaps: "Why [common practice] is costing you [thing]" |
| Cognitive fluency | Short paragraphs (3-4 sentences max); scannable subheadings every 200-300 words |
| Progressive disclosure | Most important point first; details follow |

**Length guidelines:**
- Headline: 6-12 words
- Meta description: 120-160 characters
- Subheading: 4-10 words
- Paragraph: 40-80 words

**Anti-patterns:**
- Clickbait headlines that don't deliver (erodes trust)
- Wall-of-text paragraphs (no scanning structure)
- Subheadings that don't convey meaning ("Section 1", "More Info")

### Comparison

**Copy elements needed:**
- Section headline
- Comparison axis labels
- Your product callouts
- Competitor context (without naming, or with naming if user directs)

**Psychology-aligned writing patterns:**

| Psychology Skill | Writing Approach |
|------------------|-----------------|
| Cognitive biases (anchoring) | Your product column first; set the standard |
| Hick's law | Compare on 4-6 dimensions max |
| Loss aversion | Frame gaps as "missing features" for competitors |

**Anti-patterns:**
- Comparing on 10+ dimensions (overwhelming)
- Obvious bias with no substance (checkmarks everywhere for you, Xs everywhere else)

### Portfolio / Case Studies

**Copy elements needed:**
- Section headline
- Project titles
- Brief descriptions (1-2 sentences)
- Results/metrics
- Optional: client quote per project

**Psychology-aligned writing patterns:**

| Psychology Skill | Writing Approach |
|------------------|-----------------|
| Social proof | Lead with measurable results ("Increased conversions 340%") |
| Trust | Specific details (timeline, team size, technologies) over vague claims |
| Halo effect | Lead with most recognizable client or most impressive result |

**Length guidelines:**
- Project title: 3-8 words
- Description: 20-40 words
- Result metric: 5-15 words

### Onboarding

**Copy elements needed:**
- Step headlines
- Step descriptions
- Progress indicators
- Completion message

**Psychology-aligned writing patterns:**

| Psychology Skill | Writing Approach |
|------------------|-----------------|
| Progressive disclosure | One step at a time; don't show all steps upfront |
| Fogg behavior model | Each step feels small: "Just one more thing" |
| Hooked model | Reward at each step completion ("Great, you're 50% done!") |

---

### Notification / Alert

**Copy elements needed:**
- Notification title
- Message body
- Action label(s)
- Optional: dismiss label

**Psychology-aligned writing patterns:**

| Psychology Skill | Writing Approach |
|------------------|-----------------|
| Loss aversion | Urgent notifications: "You'll lose [thing] if you don't [act]" |
| Cognitive fluency | One message, one action; never bundle notifications |
| Fogg behavior model | Action button states what happens: "Save changes" not "OK" |

**Length guidelines:**
- Title: 3-8 words
- Message: 10-25 words
- Action label: 2-4 words

## Page-Type Modifiers

Apply these modifiers ON TOP of the section patterns above. They adjust tone, urgency, and focus.

### Landing Page
- Every section's copy drives toward a single conversion goal
- Urgency language in CTAs: "Start today", "Limited spots", "Join [X] others"
- Remove all secondary navigation -- minimize escape routes
- Social proof and trust sections get extra weight
- Headline must pass the "So what?" test -- if a stranger wouldn't care, rewrite

### Homepage
- Balance information delivery with engagement -- users are exploring, not converting yet
- Avoid overselling; let the product/service speak through specifics
- Navigation copy is critical -- clear, common labels
- Hero can be broader than a landing page hero (brand story vs. conversion hook)
- Feature sections should preview depth, linking to dedicated pages

### Product / Service Page
- Feature-benefit pairing is mandatory: every feature gets a "so you can [benefit]"
- Specificity over vagueness: "Processes 10,000 records per second" beats "Lightning fast"
- Comparison language is natural here (vs. alternatives, vs. old way)
- CTAs shift from "Learn more" to "Try it" or "Get started"
- Technical accuracy matters -- don't oversimplify to the point of being wrong

### Content / Blog Page
- Readability first: Flesch-Kincaid grade 8-10
- Scannable subheadings every 200-300 words -- each subheading should make sense standalone
- Minimal sales language -- informational tone builds trust for later conversion
- Internal linking copy should be contextual, not "click here"
- Cap psychology application at 2 skills to maintain editorial authenticity

### Checkout / Signup Page
- Friction-reducing microcopy near every form field: "We'll never share your email"
- Progress indicators with encouraging copy: "Almost there!" at final step
- Reassurance language near payment fields: "256-bit encryption", "30-day money-back guarantee"
- Error messages that help, not blame: "That email doesn't look right -- try again?" not "Invalid email"
- Remove all non-essential copy -- every word that isn't helping is hurting

## Universal Anti-Patterns

These apply across ALL section types and page types:

1. **Generic openers** -- "Welcome to our website", "We are a leading...", "In today's fast-paced world..."
2. **Buzzword soup** -- "Synergistic, paradigm-shifting, best-in-class solutions"
3. **Passive voice in CTAs** -- "Your account can be created" vs. "Create your account"
4. **Exclamation mark abuse** -- One per page maximum. Zero is fine.
5. **Placeholder-quality copy** -- "Lorem ipsum" mindset where every line could apply to any business
6. **Feature-only descriptions** -- Features without benefits are specs, not copy
7. **Walls of text** -- If a section doesn't scan in 3 seconds, it needs restructuring
8. **Inconsistent voice** -- Formal in one section, casual in another (unless deliberately tiered)
